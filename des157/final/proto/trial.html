Game Design UX Best Practices

By Amir Dori

The article shares with the reader how to implement design to its best performance in order to enhance a user’s gaming experience based on proven data. Not only is the user’s gaming experience focused, the efficient actions that lead to monetization are also emphasized. A notable best practice that caught my attention is to avoid the “X” button altogether for pop ups and use the “Close” button instead. Users are usually impatient and will instantly close the pop up content without even looking at the content if there is an “X” button. Thus, using buttons like the “Close” or “Cancel” button will increase the probability that users will read the content.

Another interesting design pattern to implement in game design is the consistency of positioning positive user decision on the right and negative user decision on the left. This functions best when paired with a consistent color. For instance, green is for positive user interaction and red for negative. Consistency in design is important for users get used to something, which will help increase their user experience. Sliders are a great design choice to utilize if there are more content to offer than what is allowed to be displayed on screen. There are several informative practices offered in this article.
